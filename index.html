Lead Engineering Manager - Boost
2016 - 2017
Download the app

Boost was launched in 2017 by BCG and Axiata Digital, a subsidiary of Axiata Group Berhad, one of the largest telecommunications companies in Southeast Asia. Since its introduction, Boost has made a remarkable impact and has become one of the leading digital wallets in Malaysia.

I was a founding member of the Boost team, responsible for hiring the engineering workforce in Kuala Lumpur, worked as a software engineer on the project, and got to hit the submit button on the initial Android app release to Google Play store in December 2016.

Since its release, Boost has grown to over 5 million active users and significantly contributed to financial inclusion in Malaysia, empowering individuals who previously had limited access to banking services.

Engineering Manager - Ovo
2015 - 2016
Download the app

Initially owned by the Lippo Group, and co-founded with BCG, Ovo started as a loyalty app, which has enjoyed significant success since its 2015 launch. Ovo now provides solutions for mobile payments, peer-to-peer transfers, bill payments and has become a dominant player in the Indonesian digital payments landscape. In October 2019 it became Indonesia’s fifth unicorn.

I was part of the founding BCG team that started Ovo in the Spring of 2015, then still a fledgling app. I spent 9 months in Jakarta, Indonesia bootstrapping the business, hiring an engineering team and also building a prototype that integrated the app with Lippo’s existing core banking system (Bank Nobu), using their e-money license.

This was BCG’s first overseas incubation project, and we hired a lot of the Sydney team to staff Ovo.

Chief Technology Officer - Sypht
2018 - 2020

Sypht is an AI-based OCR product that offers intelligent processing and data extraction from documents. The company is a pioneer in the AI SAAS space, and within its first year of operation was processing millions of invoices for MYOB Australia in real-time.

I was a founding member of the Sypht team and was heavily involved in the development of its first prototype. After a successful funding round led by BPAY and BCG, I joined the company full-time as CTO, hired a data science and engineering team in Sydney and grew the platform from the initial prototype to the first very large production customer.

About Me

I’ve been programming since childhood, and have spent most of my professional career doing so. Everything started on a Commodore 64 with BASIC and whatever little assembly I could figure out by myself. Over time, I’ve had to pick up many different languages including C, Cobol, RPG, Java, C#, Javascript, Python, Swift, and fast forward to the 2020, Golang.

I have an interest in software telemetry data, quality metrics, measuring team performance and worked on a tool that visualizes contributions to github projects (unfortunately not currently available open source).

More recently, and after working on a Telco startup that required thousands of transactions per second, I developed an interest in micro-service architecture and kubernetes. With some of the lessons learned about inter-service communication, load balancing, session timeouts, HTTP and networks, my current pet project was born, and a kubernetes ingress controller for it is under development.
